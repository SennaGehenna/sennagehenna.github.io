The Curse of Strahd

By the time you arrive, night has fallen. The Vistani have proven to be as talkative as they're hospitable and whisked away the hours of your journey with tales of heroes long forgotten.

As you climb out of one of the carts, you catch a glimpse of a group of Vistani sitting around an appropriately sized bonfire, singing and laughing, visibly drunk.

„Well, 'tis our camp. It ain't much, but it's as close to a real home as it gets,“ Stanimir says, „best you visit Madam Eva in her tent. You can't miss it, it's twice as large as them others“ and so you do.

Magic flames cast a reddish glow over the interior of the large pavilion, revealing a low table covered in a black velvet cloth. Glints of light seem to flash from a a crystal ball on the table as a hunched figure peers into its depths. As the crone speaks, her voice crackles like dry weeds.

„At last you have arrived!“ Crackling laughter bursts like mad lightning from her withered lips. „Many years I have waited for the arrival of worthy heroes and here you stand. Naivara, whose wits are second to none. Donaar, whose skill at arms is so far-famed that even in our distant valley of Barovia, we've taken notice of it. Our need is great, for evil forces have taken hold in our home.“

„Well, here we are, what do want from us?“ asks Donaar, impatiently tapping his foot on the floor.

„I wish for you to know the future the cards have in store.“

With that, Madam Eva starts to lay out her cards in a peculiar fashion.

„This card tells you of history. Knowledge of the ancient will help you better understand your enemy.“ Eva turns a card and speaks: „Find the Mad Dog's crypt. The treasure lies within, beneath blackened bones.“

„Now you shall learn of a force for good and protection, a holy symbol of great hope.“ Madam Eva smirks to herself, as she intones „The treasure is with a giant whose heart is golden. His name tells tale of an unfathomable wonder.“

„A tale of power and strength and a weapon of vengeance, the sword of sunlight. You will find what you seek in the castle, amid the ruins of a place of supplication.“

As Naivara flutes „sounds like a chapel to me“, the old woman shoots her a glance that would kill a lesser being.

„To triumph against the dark, you must first find the light. Look for a dusk elf living among the Vistani. He has suffered a great loss and is haunted by dark dreams. Help him, and he will help you in return.“

„And finally, a creature of darkness, whose powers are beyond mortality. He lurks in the darkness where the morning light once shone – a sacred place.“

„And since you didn't write that down, here's the script.“ Madam Eva hands our heroes a slip of paper, which perfectly reiterates everything that has happened in the tent.

Slightly perplexed, the unlikely duo leaves the tent to sit at the bonfire, where a Vistana is telling a story our heroes have not heard before.

A might wizard came to this land over a year ago. I remember him like it was yesterday. He stood exactly where you're standing. A very charismatic man, he was. He thought he could rally the people of Barovia against the devil Strahd. He stirred them with thoughts of revolt and bore them to the castle en masse.

When the vampire appeared, the wizard's peasant army fled in terror. A few stood their ground and were never seen against.

The wizard and the vampire fought each other. Their battle flew from the courtyards of Ravenloft to a precipice overlooking the falls. I saw it with my own eyes. Thunder shook the mountainside and a great rock tumbled down upon the wizard, yet the wizard knew the names of many things and when he told the rock to break, it broke. Lightning from the heavens struck the wizard, but he told the lightning to go fuck itself with a large cactus and so it did. But when the devil Strahd fell upon him, the wizard's power couldn't save him. I saw him thrown a thousand feet to his death. When I climbed down to the river to search for the wizard's body, to, you know, figure out whether he had anything interesting on him, the River Ivlis had already spirited him away.

You go to sleep with the vague feeling, that the wizard wasn't as vulgar as the Vistana had made him out to be, and awake to an overcast morning.

Stanimir informs you that his wagons will stay for the next month and that you will need to travel by yourself for the time being. You decide to walk out the rode, as it seems pointless to wander the footpath by the river. After an hour of walking, you come to a great stone bridge. To each side, a gargoyle squats on an elevated platform and the feeling of dread is almost enough to make you turn around. On the middle of the bridge, you peer over its side and make out a magnificent waterfall, pouring down a thousand feet into the depths.

Just as you reach the other end of the bridge, a terrible downpour begins to fall from the skies. Further on ahead, you see an exquisitely crafted carriage, drawn by two black horses. As you hurry towards it, its side doors swing open silently. Wanting to get out off the rain, you step inside.
